About Me

Hello! I'm Hundaol Abera Fita. I grew up in a small yet vibrant city called Modjo. I completed my elementary education at Glorious Primary School, where I graduated with recognition from the city administration due to my exceptional performance in the national exams. This achievement opened the door for me to attend the prestigious Oromia Development Association Boarding School, where top students from across the Oromia region come together.

At this school, I not only learned how to live independently but also developed meaningful social connections with my peers. One of my greatest passions is programming. My journey into the world of coding began with the Addis Coder program, which helped me discover my potential as a programmer. I still remember the excitement of writing my very first line of code—print("Hello, world!")—and how it sparked my curiosity to learn more about algorithms and data structures.

Since then, I've been diving deeper into the programming world. Together with a group of like-minded students, I co-founded Addis Coder Spark, a program designed to inspire and encourage young students in my school to explore the endless possibilities of coding and technology.
